United States Federal Government Resources: U.S. Supreme Court

Introduction

The Supreme Court is the only court mentioned in the Constitution, and is the highest level in the three-tiered Judicial Branch.  The Supreme Court hears appeals from the courts of appeals and from the highest level state courts.  These appeals generally involve federal law or Constitutional matter. The Supreme Court has complete control over the cases that it chooses to review.  Also, Supreme Court justices are appointed by the President, and are appointed for life.

This page offers an introduction to the Supreme Court and Supreme Court research.  For more in depth legal research see:

  • Law Research Guide
  • Gallagher Law Library

About the Supreme Court

  • Supreme Court of the United States
    Official website of the Supreme Court. Find links to Supreme Court files, opinions, and recent decisions.
  • Guide to the U.S. Supreme Court
    Congressional Quarterly's guide to the U.S. Supreme Court
  • Supreme Court Nominations: The Nomination & Confirmation Process
    Gallagher Law Library guide to hearings, articles, books, CRS reports and more.

Supreme Court Decisions

  • Hein Online
    • This link opens in a new window
    For full text of reports, go to Browse Databases By Name -> US Supreme Court Library -> Official Reports -> United States Reports. [v. 1-563 (1754-2011) available]
  • United States Reports - Library of Congress
    Online searchable PDFs, v. 1-542
  • United States Reports: Cases Adjudged in the Supreme Court / Preliminary Print
    Print resource; v. 58 - 107 (1854 - 1882)
  • United States Reports: Cases Adjudged in the Supreme Court
    Print resource, v. 108 - 550 (1882 - Present) *Incomplete*
  • Supreme Court: Bound Volumes
    Full text of US Reports v.502 - Present (1991 - five years ago)
  • Supreme Court Decisions (FLITE) Bulk Data (1937 - 1975)
    FLITE allows users to download an entire database of U.S. Supreme Court decisions for free.
  • Findlaw: U.S. Supreme Court Decisions
    Searchable database of Supreme Court decisions, 1893 to present. Browsable by year and U.S. Reports volume number, and searchable by citation, case title and full text.
  • Reports of Decisions in the Supreme Court of the United States
    Print resource, 1790 - 1854.
  • Supreme Court Reporter
    Print resource, 1882 - 1982.
  • United States Supreme Court Reports
    Print resource, 1956 - 1982.

Starting Points

  • Gallagher Law Library
    The on-campus experts for legal research--visit the website, or visit in person. Try the 'Research Guides' tab for clear, well organized guides created to help begin your research and to serve as a reminder as you continue researching.
  • Law Research Guide
    Created by Gallagher Law Library. A great resource for beginning legal research.
  • Supreme Court Collection: Cornell University Law School
    Use this site to find (some FULL TEXT) historic decisions, decisions from 1992 - Present, as well as information about the court and justices.
  • Supreme Court Debates, a Pro & Con Monthly
    A pro & con monthly publication from a private company, Congressional Digest
